Block Group Selection - Instructions

Welcome to the Block Group Selection tool! Follow the steps below to identify and assess areas with challenges in computer science education. You will select block groups, choose a relevant challenge, and then verify your selections before submitting.

Steps to Complete the Selection:

  • Step 1: Select a block group on the map that you believe faces challenges in computer science education.
  • Step 2: Choose a challenge from the list provided below that you think best represents the issue in the selected block group.
  • Step 3: After selecting a challenge, repeat the process for each block group you wish to assess.
  • Step 4: Once you've selected a challenge for all block groups, review your selections.
  • Step 5: If everything looks good, click the "Submit" button to finalize your selections.

Challenges in Computer Science Education:

Limited Access to Qualified Teachers: There is a shortage of certified computer science teachers in many districts, making it challenging to offer comprehensive courses.
Resource Constraints: Some schools may lack the necessary resources, such as up-to-date technology, to effectively teach computer science.
Curriculum Integration: Integrating computer science into the existing curriculum can be difficult as schools prioritize other subjects due to standardized testing.
Awareness and Interest: There may be a lack of awareness or interest among students and parents about the importance of computer science education and its career opportunities.
Policy and Support: Initiatives like CS4GA are aimed at improving computer science education, but consistent policy support and funding are crucial.
Equity and Access to Opportunities: Many students in rural and underserved districts lack access to computer science courses, creating a digital divide.
Diversity and Inclusion: Underrepresented groups, including students from low-income backgrounds, minority communities, and girls, often have less exposure to computer science education.
Technological Infrastructure Challenges: Some districts lack the necessary technology (e.g., computers, reliable internet) to support computer science instruction.
Grant and Funding Opportunities: Identifying low-exposure areas allows schools to apply for state and federal grants aimed at expanding CS education programs.
More Specific Reasons: You are also encouraged to give more specific reasons, if you have any.
